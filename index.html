Victor Paul Merhaut-Kochman

1945

Born Vitezslav Pavel[1] in the aftermath of World War II to Alois (b. circa 1916-1917 in Vienna)[2] and his mother (b. 1916 in Mannheim).[3]

ca. 1947-50

His sister is born.[4]

1960-1961

Competes in FIM Road Racing World Championship. Entries included German Grand Prix at Solitude and the East German Grand Prix at Sachsenring (pictured)[5]

1962

Completes Abitur; works as a tour guide for German tourists in Eastern Europe and the Soviet Union.[6]

1964-1966

Marries at age 19 and becomes father to his first child, a daughter.[7]

1966

Earns a degree in Economics from Johannes Gutenberg University Mainz.[8]

1966-1969

Works in some capacity for Japan External Trade Organization.

1969-1971

Works for Pan American World Airways in Frankfurt as a cargo agent after a brief role for Lufthansa. Present for first world tour of the 747 jumbo jet; volunteers to travel to Vietnam to earn sponsorship for immigration to the United States.[9]

February 1971

Flies to New York City to begin a new life in the United States.[10]

Immigration & Naturalization Service issue him an anglicized name, Victor Paul.

He travels to Florida in search of a job with Pan Am at Tampa International Airport, but was advised to go to Los Angeles instead.[11]

Notes

1. ^ Victor was likely born in Czechoslovakia , but he was raised in Germany and spoke German as his first language. This is corroborated by his immigration papers, which state that he was born in what is now the Czech Republic, as well as a passport that the Author has seen. The Author has not seen his birth certificate.

Victor's grandfather, Paul, was a career military man and served for Germany in both World Wars, including the Afrika Korps under Erwin Rommel . He died of pneumonia when Victor was a child.

Victor remembered summers at his grandparents' farm; a chicken that would follow him around, his mother struggling to dispatch a headless duck, and exploring the nearby woods.

2. ^ Alois was an officer in the Wehrmacht and served in France for much of the war. He was captured by the Americans and held as a prisoner of war in 1945. A classically trained musician and opera baritone, he resumed his career after the war and performed in the Deutsche Oper Berlin and the Oper Frankfurt.

3. ^ To the Author's knowledge, Yarmil was older than Alois by a few months.

4. ^ To the Author's recollection.

5. ^ He was introduced to motorcycles by his uncle on his mother's side, who had several daughters but no sons. Victor was critically injured in a crash at Brno Circuit in 1961 that left him with reduced mobility in his left leg. His mother did not speak to her brother for several years after the accident.

6. ^ Victor visited the Soviet Union five times, as well as other Eastern Bloc countries. One of his favorite stories was about a German woman who insisted that she take several kilos of Hungarian peaches on board the train and was subsequently eating peaches for the rest of the trip.

7. ^ Victor never shared the name of his first wife; the Author only knows that she was from Bari, Italy, and her parents owned a company that made baby clothes. The Author has never met his half-sister.

8. ^ His resume states that he earned a bachelors degree in Economics from Johannes Gutenberg University Mainz in 1966. It erroneously listed Mainz as being in Hesse, rather than Rhineland-Pfalz.

9. ^ According to Victor, Pan Am flew to Tan Son Nhut Air Base to transport cargo and personnel. He was shocked to witness shelling on the base and realized that he was in a war zone. He was sponsored by US Representative Barry Goldwater, Jr. (R-CA 27th District) and because of his employment, eschewed military transport for a first class seat on a Pan Am 747. Victor said he only had to pay airport taxes at London Heathrow (about $10 in 1971), then flew to New York City JFK.

10. ^ He arrived in New York on February 3 in below freezing temperatures. When he arrived in Los Angeles, it was about 60 degrees F. Victor recounted an impromptu tour of Manhattan by an unscrupulous cabbie, an attempted mugging on a subway with other Europeans (it was thwarted by a plainclothes officer who took a seat next to them), and the largest rat he had ever seen on a restaurant table.

11. ^ With Pan Am reducing its operations in the 1970s, Victor did not know quite what to do. A fellow German ex-pat, Mr. Kirsch, helped him find a job at Bank of America's international division in downtown Los Angeles.

12. ^ Sunset Gower Studios was owned by Saul Pick and producer Nick Vanoff . Day-to-day operations were handled by Nick's brother, Boris. Victor employed several guards to cover shifts 24/7 as well as off duty LAPD officers. He was responsible for security at the studio and the parking lot across the street.
